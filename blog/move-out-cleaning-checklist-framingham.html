Move-Out Cleaning Checklist: Get Your Security Deposit Back

December 25, 2024 6 min read

Moving is stressful enough without worrying about your security deposit. Massachusetts law gives landlords 30 days to return your deposit, but a thorough move-out clean can help ensure you get the full amount back.

Massachusetts Security Deposit Laws

Understanding your rights helps you know what to focus on:

  • Landlords cannot charge for normal wear and tear
  • They must provide itemized list of deductions
  • Maximum deposit is first month, last month, security (one month), and lock change fee
  • Interest must be paid annually on deposits

Room-by-Room Cleaning Checklist

Kitchen

  • Oven: Clean inside and out (run self-clean cycle if available)
  • Stovetop: Remove burners/grates and clean underneath
  • Range hood: Clean filter and exterior
  • Refrigerator: Clean inside, including drawers and shelves; clean coils behind
  • Dishwasher: Clean filter, run empty cycle with vinegar
  • Microwave: Clean inside and out
  • Cabinets: Wipe inside and out, clean tops
  • Counters: Clean and sanitize
  • Sink: Clean, including faucet and drain
  • Floor: Sweep and mop, clean baseboards

Bathrooms

  • Toilet: Clean inside, outside, behind, and base
  • Tub/Shower: Scrub tile, grout, fixtures, and door/curtain rod
  • Sink: Clean basin, faucet, and drain
  • Mirror: Streak-free clean
  • Cabinets: Clean inside and out
  • Exhaust fan: Dust and clean cover
  • Floor: Mop, including behind toilet

Bedrooms & Living Areas

  • Walls: Clean scuff marks and spot clean stains
  • Closets: Vacuum and wipe shelves, rods
  • Windows: Clean glass inside and out, sills, and tracks
  • Blinds/Curtain rods: Dust or clean
  • Light fixtures: Dust and clean globes
  • Ceiling fans: Clean blades and housing
  • Outlets/Switch plates: Wipe clean
  • Baseboards: Dust and wipe
  • Floors: Vacuum carpet or mop hard floors

Entire Apartment

  • Air vents: Remove and clean or vacuum
  • Doors: Clean both sides, including frames
  • Door handles: Wipe clean
  • Replace items: Light bulbs, batteries in smoke detectors
  • Repair: Fill small nail holes with spackle

Common Deduction Triggers

Focus extra attention on these areas that often lead to deductions:

  • Oven interior: One of the most common deductions
  • Refrigerator cleanliness: Including behind and underneath
  • Bathroom grout and caulking: Mold stains are problematic
  • Carpet stains: Pet stains especially
  • Holes in walls: Beyond normal picture hanging
  • Window tracks: Often forgotten and dirty

Timeline Tips

  • 2 weeks before: Start decluttering and disposing of items
  • 1 week before: Pack and begin cleaning non-essential areas
  • Move day: Remove all belongings
  • Day after: Final deep clean when unit is empty

Document Everything

  • Take photos/video of entire unit after cleaning
  • Include timestamps
  • Compare to move-in photos
  • Keep copies of cleaning receipts if hiring professionals

A thorough move-out clean takes time but can save you hundreds of dollars in deposit deductions. When in doubt, professional cleaning services can ensure nothing is missed.
