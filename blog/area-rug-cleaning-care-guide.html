Area Rug Cleaning & Care: Protecting Your Investment

October 28, 2024 6 min read

Area rugs add warmth and character to Massachusetts homes, from antique Orientals passed down through generations to modern accent pieces. Proper care protects your investment and keeps them beautiful for years to come.

Types of Area Rugs

Hand-Knotted Oriental/Persian

  • Often valuable antiques or investments
  • Made from wool, silk, or cotton
  • Require professional cleaning
  • Can last generations with proper care

Synthetic Rugs

  • Nylon, polyester, or polypropylene
  • More stain-resistant
  • Easier to clean at home
  • Less affected by humidity

Natural Fiber Rugs

  • Jute, sisal, seagrass
  • Water-sensitive
  • Vacuum only - avoid wet cleaning
  • Best in low-traffic areas

Regular Maintenance

Vacuuming

  • Vacuum both sides regularly
  • Use low suction for delicate rugs
  • Avoid beater bars on fringed rugs
  • Vacuum with the pile direction
  • Weekly for high-traffic areas, bi-weekly otherwise

Rotation

  • Rotate 180° every 6 months
  • Prevents uneven wear and fading
  • Especially important for sunny rooms

Pad Underneath

  • Prevents slipping
  • Reduces wear
  • Adds cushioning
  • Replace pads every 5-7 years

Spot Cleaning

  1. Act immediately - blot, don't rub
  2. Blot from outside edge toward center
  3. Test cleaning solution in hidden area first
  4. Use cool water (hot sets some stains)
  5. Dry thoroughly to prevent mildew

Massachusetts-Specific Concerns

Winter Salt

  • Remove shoes before walking on rugs
  • Place rugs away from direct entry paths during winter
  • If salt reaches rug, vacuum immediately then clean

Humidity

  • Natural fiber rugs can absorb moisture and mildew
  • Ensure adequate airflow underneath
  • Don't place in damp basements

Moth Prevention

  • Vacuum regularly (moths hate disturbance)
  • Don't store rugs in plastic (traps moisture)
  • Inspect stored rugs periodically

When to Call Professionals

  • Valuable or antique rugs (always)
  • Silk or delicate fibers
  • Pet urine or biological stains
  • Severe staining or odor
  • Annual deep cleaning for high-traffic rugs
  • Before storage

Storage Tips

  • Clean before storing
  • Roll (never fold) with pile facing inward
  • Wrap in breathable fabric, not plastic
  • Store in climate-controlled space
  • Don't place items on top

DIY vs Professional

DIY appropriate:

  • Synthetic rugs
  • Small spot cleaning
  • Regular vacuuming

Professional recommended:

  • Valuable Orientals and antiques
  • Silk rugs
  • Large stains or water damage
  • Annual deep cleaning

With proper care, quality area rugs can last for generations, becoming more valuable over time. Invest in their maintenance and they'll reward you with beauty for years to come.
