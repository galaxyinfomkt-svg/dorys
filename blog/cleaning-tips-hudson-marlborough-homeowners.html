Cleaning Tips for Hudson & Marlborough Homeowners

November 18, 2024 6 min read

As a cleaning company headquartered in Marlborough, we know Hudson and Marlborough inside and out. From the historic homes downtown to new construction in growing neighborhoods, here are our local insights for keeping your home spotless.

About Our Towns

Hudson and Marlborough are classic MetroWest Massachusetts communities with a wonderful mix of:

  • Historic 18th and 19th century homes in town centers
  • 1950s-1970s ranches and capes in established neighborhoods
  • Newer subdivisions and townhomes
  • Active families, commuters, and long-time residents

Challenges Specific to Our Area

Route 20 Corridor

  • Higher traffic means more road dust
  • Heavy salt use in winter on main routes
  • Consider more frequent window cleaning

Lake and Pond Areas

  • Homes near Lake Boon or Fort Meadow Reservoir experience higher humidity
  • More vigilant mold and mildew prevention needed
  • Window condensation more common

Well Water Homes

  • Many older properties have well water
  • Higher iron content can stain fixtures
  • Hard water buildup requires regular attention

Historic Home Care

Downtown Hudson and parts of Marlborough have beautiful old homes:

Common Features

  • Wide plank hardwood floors
  • Original moldings and trim
  • Plaster walls
  • Coal or oil heat history (may have residual dust)

Cleaning Approach

  • Gentle products on original finishes
  • Careful attention to detailed woodwork
  • Address drafty areas that collect dust
  • Monitor basements for moisture issues

New Construction Considerations

Growing areas like Marlborough Hills or Hudson's newer developments:

  • Construction dust may linger for months after move-in
  • Change HVAC filters frequently the first year
  • New appliances need initial deep clean
  • Open-concept layouts require different cleaning strategies

Seasonal Tips for MetroWest

Winter

  • Route 20, I-495, and I-290 get heavy salt - manage entrance areas
  • Keep rock salt and shoe brush at doors
  • Clean furnace filters monthly

Spring

  • Pollen from orchards and farms in surrounding areas
  • April showers mean muddy entrance tracking
  • Lake area homes may have spring flood concerns

Summer

  • Beach and lake trips track in sand
  • Grill season means grease management
  • AC filters need attention

Fall

  • Beautiful foliage means leaves everywhere
  • Gutters clog quickly - affects window cleanliness
  • Prepare mudroom for winter
