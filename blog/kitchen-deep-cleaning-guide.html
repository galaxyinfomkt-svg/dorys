Kitchen Deep Cleaning Guide: Beyond the Surface

December 18, 2024 8 min read

The kitchen is the heart of the home - and often the room that accumulates the most grime. This guide covers professional techniques for a truly deep clean that goes beyond everyday tidying.

The Top-to-Bottom Approach

Always clean from top to bottom so debris falls onto surfaces you haven't cleaned yet:

  1. Ceiling and light fixtures
  2. Upper cabinets and tops of cabinets
  3. Walls and backsplash
  4. Appliances
  5. Lower cabinets and counters
  6. Floors

Appliance Deep Cleaning

Oven

  • Self-cleaning ovens: Run the cycle, then wipe ash when cool
  • Manual cleaning: Apply baking soda paste, leave overnight, scrub and wipe
  • Racks: Soak in hot soapy water, scrub with steel wool
  • Glass door: Make paste of baking soda, apply between glass panels
  • Don't forget: Broiler drawer, exterior, and knobs

Refrigerator

  • Remove all food and check expiration dates
  • Remove shelves and drawers, wash in warm soapy water
  • Wipe interior with baking soda solution (2 tbsp per quart of water)
  • Clean door gaskets with toothbrush
  • Vacuum condenser coils underneath or behind
  • Empty and clean drip pan

Dishwasher

  • Remove and clean filter
  • Clean spray arms (unclog holes with toothpick)
  • Wipe door edges and gasket
  • Run empty cycle with 2 cups white vinegar
  • Follow with baking soda cycle

Microwave

  • Heat bowl of water with lemon for 5 minutes to steam
  • Wipe interior easily
  • Remove and wash turntable
  • Clean exterior and vent

Cabinet Deep Clean

Exterior

  • Remove grease buildup with dish soap solution
  • For stubborn grease: oil soap or degreaser
  • Clean hardware (remove if heavily soiled)
  • Don't forget tops of upper cabinets (major dust collectors)

Interior

  • Empty completely
  • Vacuum crumbs
  • Wipe with all-purpose cleaner
  • Line with fresh shelf paper if desired
  • Check for expired items before replacing

Tackling Grease

Kitchen grease accumulates everywhere:

Range Hood

  • Soak metal filters in hot water with dish soap and baking soda
  • Scrub with brush
  • Clean hood exterior and light covers
  • Wipe fan if accessible

Backsplash

  • Spray with degreaser
  • Let sit 5 minutes
  • Scrub grout with brush
  • Wipe clean

Sink and Faucet

  • Stainless steel: Scrub with baking soda, rinse, dry with grain
  • Porcelain: Use non-abrasive cleaner
  • Drain: Pour baking soda followed by vinegar, flush with hot water
  • Faucet: Remove mineral deposits with vinegar-soaked cloth
  • Sprayer: Soak in vinegar to unclog

High-Touch Surfaces

Sanitize these germ hotspots:

  • Light switches
  • Cabinet handles
  • Refrigerator handle
  • Faucet handles
  • Appliance buttons/touchscreens
  • Drawer pulls

Floor Deep Clean

  • Move appliances and furniture if possible
  • Sweep or vacuum thoroughly
  • Scrub baseboards
  • Mop with appropriate cleaner for your floor type
  • Clean under refrigerator (dust affects efficiency)

How Often to Deep Clean

  • Oven: Every 3-6 months
  • Refrigerator: Every 3 months
  • Dishwasher: Monthly
  • Range hood filters: Monthly
  • Cabinet interiors: Every 6 months
  • Full deep clean: Seasonally or as needed

A deep-cleaned kitchen isn't just more pleasant to cook in - it's also more hygienic and can even improve appliance efficiency and longevity.
