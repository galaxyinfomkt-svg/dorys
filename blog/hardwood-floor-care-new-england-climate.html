Floor Care

Hardwood Floor Care in New England's Changing Climate

December 22, 2024 5 min read

New England's dramatic seasonal shifts create unique challenges for hardwood floors. Understanding how humidity affects wood and adapting your care routine seasonally will help preserve your floors for decades.

How Climate Affects Hardwood

Wood is hygroscopic - it absorbs and releases moisture based on its environment:

  • Summer: High humidity causes wood to expand
  • Winter: Dry heated air causes wood to contract
  • Result: Seasonal movement can cause gaps, cupping, or crowning

Seasonal Care Guide

Winter (December-March)

The biggest threat to Massachusetts hardwood floors:

  • Problem: Forced air heating drops humidity to 20-30%
  • Result: Gaps between boards, possible cracking
  • Solution: Run humidifier to maintain 35-55% humidity
  • Salt protection: Place mats at all entrances, clean salt immediately
  • Snow/water: Never let standing water remain on floors

Spring (April-May)

  • Gradually reduce humidifier use as outdoor humidity rises
  • Deep clean floors to remove salt residue from winter
  • Watch for gaps closing as humidity increases
  • Mud season: Enhanced entrance mat protocol

Summer (June-August)

  • Problem: Humidity can exceed 70%
  • Result: Cupping (edges higher than center of boards)
  • Solution: Use AC or dehumidifier to maintain reasonable levels
  • Watch for sticky or swollen doors/windows as indicators

Fall (September-November)

  • Ideal time for floor refinishing if needed
  • Prepare humidifier for winter use
  • Deep clean before heating season

Daily and Weekly Care

Daily

  • Sweep or dust mop high-traffic areas
  • Wipe up spills immediately
  • Use felt pads under furniture

Weekly

  • Vacuum with hard floor attachment (no beater bar)
  • Damp mop with hardwood-safe cleaner
  • Check humidity levels

Cleaning Products to Use

  • pH-neutral hardwood floor cleaners
  • Microfiber mops and cloths
  • Manufacturer-recommended products for your finish type

Products to Avoid

  • Water and vinegar: Can dull finish over time
  • Steam mops: Heat and moisture damage wood
  • Oil soap: Builds up residue
  • Wax on polyurethane: Creates slippery, cloudy surface
  • Wet mops: Too much water is enemy of wood

Signs Your Floors Need Professional Attention

  • Dull, worn finish
  • Persistent scratches
  • Warping or cupping that doesn't resolve
  • Water damage
  • Deep stains

Historic Home Considerations

Many Massachusetts homes have original wide-plank floors:

  • These floors have lived through centuries of climate changes
  • Expect some seasonal movement - it's normal
  • Use natural wax finishes rather than polyurethane when appropriate
  • Consult preservation specialists for significant issues

With proper seasonal care, your hardwood floors will remain beautiful through New England's dramatic climate swings for generations to come.
