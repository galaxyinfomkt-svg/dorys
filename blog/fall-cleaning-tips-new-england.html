Fall Cleaning Tips: Preparing for New England Winters

November 28, 2024 5 min read

New England's beautiful fall foliage signals that winter is coming. This is the perfect time to prepare your home for months of cold weather, snow, and spending more time indoors. A thorough fall cleaning sets you up for a comfortable winter.

Why Fall Cleaning Matters in Massachusetts

  • Last chance to deep clean before holiday season
  • Prepare for months of closed windows
  • Address outdoor-to-indoor transition zones
  • Prevent pest entry before they seek winter shelter
  • Optimize heating efficiency

Indoor Fall Cleaning Checklist

HVAC and Heating

  • Replace or clean HVAC filters
  • Clean air vents and returns
  • Schedule furnace maintenance
  • Clean baseboard heaters
  • Test heating system before you need it

Windows and Doors

  • Wash windows inside and out (last chance before freezing temps)
  • Clean window tracks and sills
  • Check weather stripping
  • Clean and store screens
  • Install storm windows

Deep Cleaning Tasks

  • Clean behind and under furniture
  • Vacuum and rotate mattresses
  • Wash or dry clean heavy bedding
  • Deep clean carpets and area rugs
  • Clean upholstery
  • Dust ceiling fans and light fixtures

Kitchen Prep

  • Clean oven before holiday cooking
  • Clean refrigerator inside and out
  • Organize pantry and check expiration dates
  • Clean range hood and filter

Entryway and Mudroom Prep

These areas work overtime during New England winters:

  • Deep clean floors
  • Organize coat closet
  • Set up boot trays and drying racks
  • Stock with quality entrance mats
  • Create space for winter gear
  • Install hooks for wet coats

Seasonal Storage Swap

Put Away

  • Summer clothing (clean before storing)
  • Beach gear and summer toys
  • Outdoor furniture cushions
  • Screen doors

Bring Out

  • Winter coats, hats, gloves
  • Heavy blankets and throws
  • Humidifier
  • Snow removal equipment

Outdoor Tasks That Affect Indoor Cleanliness

  • Clean gutters to prevent ice dams
  • Sweep porches and entryways
  • Clean outdoor mats
  • Store or cover outdoor furniture
  • Clear leaves from window wells

Fireplace and Chimney

  • Schedule chimney cleaning/inspection
  • Clean firebox and grate
  • Check damper operation
  • Stock firewood away from house
  • Clean fireplace tools and accessories

Pest Prevention

Fall is when pests seek winter shelter:

  • Seal gaps around windows and doors
  • Check screens for holes
  • Clean up crumbs and food debris
  • Store food in airtight containers
  • Don't leave pet food out overnight

Fall Cleaning Timeline

  • September: Deep cleaning and summer storage
  • October: Outdoor tasks and heating prep
  • November: Final touches before holiday season

A thorough fall cleaning makes your home more comfortable, energy-efficient, and ready for whatever New England winter brings. Start early and tackle tasks systematically for best results.
