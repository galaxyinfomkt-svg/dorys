Declutter & Organize: The First Step to a Cleaner Home

October 8, 2024 8 min read

You can't clean clutter. Before any deep cleaning session, decluttering and organizing makes the actual cleaning faster, more thorough, and longer-lasting. Here's how to tackle the clutter so your home can truly shine.

Why Declutter Before Cleaning?

  • Access: Can't clean surfaces covered with stuff
  • Efficiency: Less to clean around means faster cleaning
  • Thoroughness: Every surface gets attention
  • Dust reduction: Less stuff = less dust traps
  • Mental clarity: Organized spaces feel cleaner longer

The Four-Box Method

For each area, prepare four containers:

  1. Keep: Items you use and love
  2. Donate: Good condition items you don't need
  3. Trash: Broken or worn-out items
  4. Relocate: Items that belong elsewhere in home

Room-by-Room Guide

Kitchen

  • Check expiration dates in pantry and fridge
  • Donate duplicate items and unused gadgets
  • Recycle old containers without lids
  • Clear counters of rarely-used appliances
  • Organize under sink

Bathrooms

  • Check medication expiration dates
  • Dispose of old cosmetics and toiletries
  • Match up towels, donate extras
  • Clear counters of daily-use items only

Bedrooms

  • Clear nightstand surfaces
  • Under bed: keep only appropriate storage
  • Sort through dresser drawers
  • Closet: if you haven't worn it in a year, consider donating

Living Areas

  • Sort through magazines and papers
  • Organize media (or go digital)
  • Clear flat surfaces
  • Organize bookshelves

Massachusetts Donation Options

Local places to donate:

  • Goodwill (multiple locations)
  • Salvation Army
  • Local churches and shelters
  • Vietnam Veterans of America (home pickup)
  • Habitat for Humanity ReStore (home items)
  • Local Buy Nothing groups on Facebook

Storage Solutions

For items you're keeping:

  • Clear bins for visibility
  • Labels for easy finding
  • Vertical storage maximizes space
  • Under-bed storage for seasonal items
  • Door-mounted organizers for small spaces

Maintaining Organization

  • One in, one out: For every new item, donate one
  • 10-minute tidy: Daily quick pickup
  • Designated spots: Everything has a home
  • Weekly resets: Return items to their places
  • Seasonal purges: Quarterly mini-declutter sessions

When Decluttering is Difficult

Emotional attachment to items is normal:

  • Take photos of sentimental items before donating
  • Start with easy categories (expired food, broken items)
  • Involve family members in decisions about shared items
  • Consider professional organizer help for overwhelming situations

Seasonal Decluttering

  • Spring: Winter gear assessment, general purge
  • Summer: Kids' toys and school supplies
  • Fall: Summer gear, closet swap
  • Winter: Pre-holiday clearing, gift preparation

The Payoff

Benefits of decluttered home:

  • Faster daily tidying
  • Easier deep cleaning
  • Reduced stress
  • Better air quality (less dust traps)
  • More space to enjoy

Decluttering is the foundation of a truly clean home. Take it one room at a time, and soon you'll enjoy a more organized, easier-to-clean living space.
