Fighting Humidity: Summer Cleaning Tips for Massachusetts Homes

November 12, 2024 5 min read

Massachusetts summers can be surprisingly humid, with July and August often seeing humidity levels above 70%. This creates unique cleaning challenges and potential problems if not addressed properly.

Humidity Problems in Massachusetts Homes

  • Mold and mildew growth, especially in bathrooms and basements
  • Musty odors
  • Sticky surfaces that attract dust
  • Condensation on windows
  • Warping of wood floors and furniture
  • Dust mites thrive in humid conditions

Controlling Indoor Humidity

Target Levels

Indoor humidity should be 30-50% for comfort and health. During humid Massachusetts summers:

  • Use AC to dehumidify (most AC units remove moisture)
  • Run dehumidifiers in basements and damp areas
  • Use exhaust fans when cooking and showering
  • Keep windows closed during high humidity days

Problem Areas

  • Basements: Most prone to humidity - run dehumidifier continuously
  • Bathrooms: Run exhaust fan during and 20 min after showers
  • Kitchen: Use range hood when cooking
  • Laundry: Vent dryer outside, don't hang dry indoors

Cleaning for Humidity Season

Daily Tasks

  • Squeegee shower walls after each use
  • Wipe bathroom surfaces to prevent moisture buildup
  • Empty dehumidifier reservoirs
  • Check under sinks for moisture

Weekly Tasks

  • Clean bathroom surfaces with mold-inhibiting products
  • Vacuum upholstered furniture (dust mites love humidity)
  • Wash bedding in hot water
  • Check basement for signs of moisture or mold

Monthly Tasks

  • Clean AC filters
  • Check and clean dehumidifier coils
  • Inspect window frames for mold
  • Clean refrigerator drip pan

Preventing Mold Growth

Mold can grow in 24-48 hours on damp surfaces:

  • Fix any leaks immediately
  • Don't leave wet items sitting
  • Keep closet doors open for air circulation
  • Use mold-resistant shower curtains
  • Clean shower curtains monthly

Odor Control

Musty smells are common in humid conditions:

  • Address the moisture source first
  • Use baking soda in problem areas
  • Clean soft furnishings that absorb odors
  • Consider air purifiers with activated carbon
  • Open windows when outdoor humidity is low

Protecting Your Home

Wood Floors

  • Maintain consistent humidity (prevents expansion/contraction)
  • Wipe up spills immediately
  • Use dehumidifier if you see cupping

Fabric and Upholstery

  • Vacuum frequently to remove dust mites
  • Consider professional cleaning mid-summer
  • Use washable slipcovers when possible

Signs You Have a Humidity Problem

  • Condensation on windows
  • Musty smell
  • Visible mold
  • Peeling paint or wallpaper
  • Warped wood
  • Increased allergy symptoms

Managing humidity is key to summer comfort and home health in Massachusetts. Stay proactive with cleaning and moisture control to enjoy a fresh, comfortable summer.
