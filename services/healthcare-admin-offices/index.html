Frequently Asked Questions About Healthcare Administrative Office Cleaning

Common questions from office managers and healthcare administrators.

Our healthcare administrative office cleaning includes workstation and desk sanitation, conference room cleaning, reception and lobby area maintenance, restroom sanitation, hard floor and carpet care, high-touch surface disinfection (door handles, elevator buttons, shared equipment), breakroom cleaning, and compliance documentation. We treat these spaces with healthcare-grade protocols because they often handle PHI and connect to clinical areas.

Most healthcare administrative offices benefit from daily cleaning, especially reception areas and shared workspaces. Offices with high visitor traffic or those co-located with clinical spaces may need more frequent attention. Dory's works with your office manager to develop a cleaning frequency that keeps the workspace healthy and professional without disrupting daily operations.

Dory's follows OSHA workplace safety standards, EPA-registered disinfectant protocols, and Massachusetts Department of Public Health guidelines. Because healthcare administrative offices often handle protected health information, our staff are trained in HIPAA-aware cleaning practices — ensuring documents, screens, and secure areas are respected during every service visit.

Healthcare administrative offices have unique needs that general commercial cleaners overlook — from HIPAA-sensitive document areas to shared spaces connecting clinical and non-clinical zones. Dory's exclusively serves the healthcare industry and brings over 22 years of experience to every facility. Our staff understand the importance of maintaining healthcare-grade cleanliness even in non-clinical spaces, and we provide the documentation and reliability that healthcare organizations require.
